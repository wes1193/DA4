An excellent move depends largely on how much you get involved and how well prepared you are when the movers arrive.  The following is a guide to assist you.   Please keep in mind, the below guide is simply a list of common questions.  Please refer to the links below for a complete set of all responsibilities.

  • On any/all days of servicing, it is required that members are available from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM per military regulations. ORIGIN:

  • It is highly recommended that your residence is in a tidy and organized condition to help the movers that are packing your property (e.g. all clothing should be laundered, dishes clean and items separated that are NOT intended for crew packing).

  • Please remember that liquids (flammables, oils, cleaning supplies, chemicals) are not authorized for shipping.  Shipping liquids could void claims liability.  Please set these aside before days of servicing and plan to ship them with you. 

  • Have your property separated and distinctly marked.  Ensure items that are PRO GEAR or items intended for extra delivery or storage (either personal or at government expense) are clearly differentiated on boxes and inventory sheets prior to signing off.  

  • Do not ship small, extremely valuable items such as stocks, bonds, jewelry, coins/coin collections, and items of great sentimental value such as photo albums. Pack them in your suitcases and hand-carry them, this also includes your purchase receipts, pictures, and appraisals.

  • Anything you’re shipping yourself needs to already be locked in a room or packed in your car to avoid the crew accidentally packing it for you. They are not required to go back and unpack boxes to find anything that was accidentally packed.

  • Separate your professional items and any authorized additional consumables.  Be sure they are identified on the inventory as “PBP&E/Pro-Gear”. Please ensure they are weighed separately or a constructive weight has been annotated on the inventory. 

  • Remove hanging objects scheduled for shipping from the walls and ceilings.  This includes curtain rods, mounted TV’s, kitchenware, mirrors and pictures, etc.  The crew is not responsible to remove anything from walls.

  • Dismantle outdoor play equipment and outdoor structures (e.g. trampolines, utility sheds, furniture, playhouses, swing or gym sets).

  • Remove personal property from an attic or crawl space and place items elsewhere for crew to pack.  The crew is not required to go into such areas to gather items.

Shipping Electronics / Appliances / Power Equipment

  • Disconnect all TV’s; un-mount them from the wall, prior to pack date.

  • Remove your TV antenna; disconnect your satellite dish

  • Disconnect and prepare all components such as stereos, compact disc players, videodisc players, computers, printers, televisions, and VCR’s for the move.

  • Make sure major items (e.g. pieces of stereo equipment) are individually noted on the inventory with complete and accurate descriptions including make, model and serial numbers. 

  • If the packers list “color television”, have them add the size, make, model, and serial number.  This procedure also applies to stereo, video or other electronic components. 

  • Closely check the string of symbols showing pre-existing damages.  These symbols are explained at the top, right hand corner of the inventory.  

  • Disconnect all appliances such as: washer, dryer, and stove.  Charges associated with disconnecting these appliances are your responsibility and should be performed prior to pack date.  If you are shipping or storing a front-load washer, please provide necessary hardware (shipping bolts) when available.  If not, please provide the make and model to the pre-move surveyor at time of the survey so we can see about assisting.  At destination, do not run the front load washer with the bolts installed. If assistance is needed to remove the bolts, please contact your Customer Care Advocate.

  • Empty, defrost, and thoroughly wash the inside of your refrigerator and/or freezer to keep mildew at a minimum during transit and storage, these appliances need at least two days to dry out.  Leave doors open after cleaning.

Engine Power Equipment (e.g. motorcycle, lawnmower, boat, etc.)

  • Ensure they are free of dirt or grease. Disconnect the battery cables and tape the leads so they do not make contact with the battery terminals. Disconnect lead from spark-plug and also tape the wire lead so it does not make contact with spark-plug. With the engine running, completely run it out of gas until it stalls.

PRIOR TO SIGNING INVENTORIES: 

  • Be sure to double check the inventory papers and make sure you agree with everything they write (condition of furniture, etc.). We want to make sure that you are confirming ALL boxes have been inventoried with a number. 

  • Please do one last walk through before signing off on the inventories and ensure that everything has been loaded into the truck.  It is your responsibility to double check that the house is clear. Anything you find that was missed after they leave will be difficult to get picked up in a timely manner.

RESIDENTIAL DAMAGE: 

  • Should any damage occur to the residence at the time of pack/load/delivery, the TSP must be notified within five (7) GBDs of the damage and provide contact information. 

DESTINATION:

  • Your shipment can arrive anytime between the Load date and the RDD (Required Delivery Date). If your shipment arrives to the destination and you are unable to accept delivery, the items will be placed in temporary storage (SIT) until you coordinate a delivery date out of storage. The RDD is NOT a guaranteed delivery date or a scheduled delivery date.

  • You will not be able to access your HHG while it’s in temporary storage.  You may be authorized a one-time pickup from the warehouse or a partial delivery. You will have to provide inventory numbers for what you want released, and you would have to take those boxes/items home with you; they cannot go back into the warehouse since the liability was voided.

  • TO SCHEDULE DELIVERY:  Please contact me to schedule, with notice if possible. Upon contacting us to schedule, please allow 48 hours for scheduling to be confirmed.

  • Outside of Peak Season:  Wait times can vary between a few days to 2 weeks.

  • During Peak Season:  Wait times can be between 2-4 weeks.

UNPACKING (Please Request Prior to Delivery):

    Full Unpack: Is a onetime placement of your items. The delivery crew will help reassemble anything that was disassembled at origin, they will also assist in removing items out of boxes and removing empty boxes from your home.
  • Uncrating: If any items were crated at origin, please retain the crates for subsequent military moves. Per the Defense Transportation Regulation, all approved crates remain the property of the customer for future use. Crates may not be authorized for the same item should you choose to dispose of them.
